# Medical Report Samples — Notes (Needs Real Medical Reports)

## What happened to the ICMR STWs?

The 10 ICMR Standard Treatment Workflow PDFs that were here have been moved to `poster/`.
They are single-page clinical flowchart posters (297×576mm), not multi-page medical reports.
See `poster/NOTES.md` for details.

## What belongs in medical_report/

True multi-page clinical documents:
- Patient discharge summaries
- Clinical case reports
- Hospital investigation reports (lab + radiology)
- Clinical guidelines (multi-page, chapter-structured)
- WHO or NICE clinical guidelines PDFs

These test different challenges than the ICMR STWs:
- Multi-page document structure
- Chapter/section hierarchy
- Drug dosage tables (tabular, not flowchart boxes)
- Clinical criteria lists in numbered format
- Footnotes with evidence levels (e.g., Grade A, Level 1b)
- Cross-references between sections

## Where to find good samples

### PubMed Central (open access clinical guidelines)
https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/
Search: "clinical practice guideline" — many are freely downloadable as PDF

### NICE Guidelines (UK) — free PDFs
https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance
Example: https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng136/resources/hypertension-in-adults-diagnosis-and-management-pdf

### WHO Clinical Guidelines — free PDFs
https://www.who.int/publications/guidelines
Example: WHO Hypertension Guidelines, WHO Diabetes Management Guidelines

### ESC (European Society of Cardiology) Guidelines — some free
https://www.escardio.org/Guidelines/Clinical-Practice-Guidelines

### NHM Standard Treatment Guidelines (India) — different from ICMR STWs
https://main.mohfw.gov.in/sites/default/files/Standard Treatment Guidelines.pdf

## What to look for

- 10–100 pages (multi-page is essential — tests heading hierarchy + continuity)
- Contains at least one data table (drug dosages, diagnostic criteria)
- Has a clear section structure (Introduction, Diagnosis, Treatment, References)
- Clinical language with abbreviations (tests text quality preservation)
